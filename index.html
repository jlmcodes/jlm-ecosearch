🔬 Unified Denominator Protocols

Because these low-cost organic products share fundamental mechanisms, their creation relies on one of these five base organic protocols rather than repetitive, unique procedures.

Protocol A: Aqueous Extraction

Used for: Biocoagulant Pills, Bio-Extracts.

Macerate the organic biomass in distilled water. To extract tannins/pigments, boil for 2 hours. Filter, then evaporate in the sun to concentrate into a thick liquid, gel, or powder (pill form).

Protocol B: Bio-Compression

Used for: Biocomposites, Bio-Bricks, Erasers.

Sun-dry fibrous waste completely. Create an organic binder by boiling cassava starch. Mix 70% fiber with 30% binder. Pack tightly into a customized mold and compress using heavy manual weights. Sun-dry for 5-7 days.

Protocol C: Low-Temp Biochar

Used for: Biochar, Bio-Desiccants, Sachets.

Place dry biomass into a sealed tin can with a single exhaust hole. Heat over an open fire until smoke emitting stops (pyrolysis). Allow to cool completely to obtain highly porous, activated biochar.

Protocol D: Bio-Textile Weaving

Used for: Filtration Mats, Sponges, Nets.

Harvest long leaves/stems and wilt them in the sun for 1-2 days to draw out moisture, increasing flexibility and preventing snapping. Braid or weave tightly into functional grids or pads.

Protocol E: Bio-Fermentation & Densification

Used for: Biofuels & Bioenergy (Solid Briquettes, Gels).

For Gels: Macerate sugar/starch-rich biomass, introduce baker's yeast, ferment anaerobically for 7 days. Distill the bio-ethanol, mix with starch to form a flammable gel.
For Solid Fuels: Dry fibrous waste, crush, mix with starch binder, and compress tightly into high-density briquettes.
